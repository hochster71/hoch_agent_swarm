## Why now — the wedge is a legal deadline, not a trend

This is the single most important thing on the page, and it is time-boxed.

- The **FY2026 NDAA (signed Dec 2025), Section 1513**, directs DoD to build a cybersecurity
  framework **specifically for AI/ML systems, as an extension of CMMC**. Status update to Congress
  was due **16 June 2026 — last month.**
- **CMMC Phase 1 enforcement is live**: 10 Nov 2025 → 9 Nov 2026. The clock is running *now*.
- **AI agents inherit every obligation of the humans they replace.** No AI exemption. An agent
  touching CUI/FCI is fully in scope for CMMC and NIST SP 800-171/800-53.
- **False Claims Act exposure.** Contractors certifying compliance without AI governance over
  CUI-touching workflows are exposed under the DOJ Civil Cyber-Fraud Initiative.

**The buying trigger is not "AI is scary." It is: *you have already deployed agents, you have
already certified, and the framework is landing on top of you inside 12 months.***

That is a CISO's problem this quarter — not a 2027 roadmap item.
