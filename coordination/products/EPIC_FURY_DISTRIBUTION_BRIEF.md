# Epic Fury — Distribution Brief

**Date:** 2026-07-14 · **Status:** honest assessment, not launch copy
**Author:** Claude (HELM council) · **Evidence:** live market research, cited below

---

## The finding I have to lead with

**Epic Fury at $19/mo is squeezed between free and enterprise, and the free tier is good.**

| Tier | Who | Price | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | working analysts, journalists, researchers | **$0** | [Liveuamap](https://liveuamap.com), [ACLED](https://acleddata.com), World Monitor, Glint |
| **Epic Fury** | ? | **$19/mo** | AI-generated analysis |
| **Enterprise** | Fortune 100 SOCs, banks, governments | **$50k–$100k/yr** | [Recorded Future](https://www.recordedfuture.com), [RANE](https://www.ranenetwork.com), [S&P Global](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/solutions/geopolitical-risk-analysis), Dataminr |

The free tools are not toys. Liveuamap gives **real-time strike reports, troop movements, air-defence activity — often ahead of official press statements.** ACLED gives free conflict data export to journalists and researchers. Analysts genuinely use these. There is a documented trend of the "homemade crisis room" — free tools replacing six-figure contracts.

**So the question a buyer will ask is:** *why pay $19/mo for AI commentary when Liveuamap gives me verified ground truth for free?*

I don't have a good answer to that yet. Neither does the current landing page.

## The second problem: what IS Epic Fury?

It currently sells two different things under one name:

1. **Live OSINT** — real-world conflict intelligence (competes with free, above)
2. **Operation Epic Fury** — a *simulated* scenario (a wargame)

We labelled every dashboard LIVE / SIMULATED / MIXED, which was the right call for truth. But as a **product** it's confusing: a buyer cannot tell in five seconds whether they're buying intelligence or a simulation. Products that require an explanation of what they are do not convert.

## The good news, and it's real

The **wargaming/simulation niche is genuinely underserved for individuals.**

- [WarPaths](https://warpaths.io) — sells decision-simulation to *organisations*, not individuals
- [Wargame Design Studio](https://wargameds.com) — 100+ titles, one-off purchases, not live scenarios
- [VASSAL](https://vassalengine.org) — free, but manual and dated
- 4C Strategies / Exonaut — enterprise military training

**Nobody is selling a live, AI-driven, continuously-updating conflict simulation to an individual for the price of a magazine.** That is an actual gap, and Epic Fury already *is* that thing — it's just wearing an intelligence-platform costume.

## Three options

### A. Reposition as a live wargame *(my recommendation)*
Stop competing with free OSINT. Own the simulation.
- "A living wargame. Operation Epic Fury updates every 15 minutes with AI-driven ORBAT, BDA, and forecasting."
- Buyer: wargamers, defence hobbyists, military-history readers, sim enthusiasts, students
- $19/mo is *cheap* for a hobby subscription and *unbeatable* vs. a $60 wargame title
- The free OSINT tools are **not competitors** — they're a different product entirely
- Keep the live-OSINT dashboards as a *feature* ("grounded in real-world data"), not the pitch

### B. Go free-tier + paid depth
Give away what Liveuamap gives away, charge for AI synthesis, forecasts, archive, export.
- Honest, but you're now in a knife fight with free tools that have years of trust
- Requires traffic you don't have

### C. Go enterprise
Charge $2k+/mo to risk desks. Different product, different sales motion, long cycles.
- Not viable solo, not soon

## What I'd do next (and most of it needs zero founder-minutes)

1. **Decide the positioning.** Only you can make this call — it's a product identity decision, and I won't make it for you.
2. Then, autonomously: rewrite the landing page for the chosen position, build the launch asset set, identify the 5–10 communities where the buyer actually lives (r/wargame, r/CredibleDefense, Hacker News, defence Discords, sim forums), and draft the launch posts.
3. **Ship one thing that gets seen.** Zero traffic is the binding constraint. Not the factory. Not the checkout. **Nobody knows this exists.**

## What I will NOT do

Write you cheerful launch copy for a positioning I think is wrong. That would be the commercial equivalent of a fake-green: a confident-looking artifact that fails on contact with reality.
