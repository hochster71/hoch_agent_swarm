What this is. Organizational tooling. It describes the file you upload — nothing more. It is not financial, tax, or legal advice, and it does not tell you which clients to keep, chase, or drop.

What you get

  • Per-client breakdown — net revenue, share of total, invoice count, average and largest invoice, first and last invoice, months active.
  • Concentration figures — top 1 / 3 / 5 / 10 share, HHI on the standard 0–10,000 scale, effective client count, and how few clients make up the first 50% and 80%.
  • Monthly revenue series — net per month, active clients, and the largest client in each month.
  • Payment timing — if your file has a paid-date column, median and slowest days-to-pay overall and per client.
  • Spelling merge — "Acme Corp", "ACME CORPORATION" and "Acme Corp." are grouped into one client, and the report tells you which spellings it merged.
  • Flagged rows — every row it could not read, with its CSV line number and the reason. Nothing is silently dropped.
  • Two files — a 5-sheet XLSX workbook and a one-page PDF summary.

Your CSV needs three columns

A date, a client name, and an amount. Common header names are recognised automatically (Invoice Date, Customer, Total, and so on). Invoice Number, Paid Date and Status are optional and add detail if present.

Try it free

The free preview runs the real engine on your file and shows the top-line figures. Your file is processed to produce the report and is not stored.

Honest limits

Client grouping is string matching only — there is no company database behind it, so two genuinely different clients with near-identical names could merge, and a heavily abbreviated name may not. Every merge it makes is listed in the report so you can check it. Figures reflect only the rows in the file you upload: revenue invoiced outside that file is invisible to it. Rows it cannot read are flagged, never guessed at.
