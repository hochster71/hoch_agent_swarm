What this is. Organizational tooling. It reports the figures in the file you upload — nothing more. It is not financial, tax, or legal advice, it does not tell you what to charge, and it does not compare you to any market.

What you get

  • Per-client breakdown — hours, share of tracked time, entries, billable vs non-billable hours, observed revenue, rate per covered hour, blended rate, median and longest session, months active, first and last entry.
  • Two honest rates — the rate on the hours that carry billing figures, and the blended rate across every tracked hour. The report tells you exactly what share of your hours had billing data.
  • Billable share — how your tracked time splits between billable and non-billable, overall and by month, when your file has a billable column.
  • Monthly + weekday series — hours, revenue and active clients per month; total hours by weekday.
  • Spelling merge — "Acme Corp" and "ACME CORPORATION" are grouped into one client, and the report tells you which spellings it merged.
  • Flagged rows — every row it could not read, with its CSV line number and the reason. Nothing is silently dropped.
  • Two files — a 5-sheet XLSX workbook and a one-page PDF summary.

Your CSV needs three columns

A date, a client name, and a duration. Durations can be decimal hours (3.5), clock form (3:30 or 3:30:00), 3h 30m, or a minutes column. Common header names are recognised automatically. Project, Billable, Amount, Rate and Notes are optional and add detail if present — when only a rate column exists, figures are computed as rate × hours from your own file.

Try it free

The free preview runs the real engine on your file and shows the top-line figures. Your file is processed to produce the report and is not stored.

Honest limits

Every figure is arithmetic over the rows in the file you upload — hours or revenue recorded outside that file are invisible to it, and there is no market-rate database behind any number. Client grouping is string matching only, so two genuinely different clients with near-identical names could merge; every merge it makes is listed so you can check it. Rows it cannot read are flagged, never guessed at. Negative billed amounts are excluded from rate figures and disclosed.
